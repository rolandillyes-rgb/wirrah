The Problem

First Nations workforce turnover is the sector's primary operational risk. The cause is being managed nowhere.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander workers carry colonial load: navigating institutional systems, managing cultural obligations alongside clinical responsibilities, and absorbing microaggressions without support or recognition. It accumulates. It is invisible to leadership. And it is the primary driver of departure in this sector.

Colonial load is a psychosocial hazard under Australian WHS legislation. Employers have a positive duty to identify it and manage it, with documented evidence of action. Awareness training does not satisfy that duty. A system does.

The Solution

Wirrah gives First Nations organisation leaders visibility of colonial load and a documented system to act on it.

Wirrah operates entirely within the Microsoft 365 environment your organisation already uses. No new platforms, no algorithms, no app to install. The CEO or MD receives a fortnightly plain-language report, prepared by Wirrah's expert advisory panel, with structured recommended actions.

The mechanism: staff contribute through a weekly culturally safe check-in and a fortnightly early signals survey. The expert advisory panel reviews the results and writes the report. Leadership gets visibility. The organisation gets a documented WHS record.

  • Fortnightly plain-language report with recommended actions, prepared by Wirrah's expert advisory panel
  • Early visibility of colonial load concentration, before it becomes a departure
  • Colonial load identified and named, not absorbed and invisible
  • A documented WHS record providing defensible evidence of psychosocial risk management
  • No new technology. Runs inside your existing Microsoft 365 tenancy

Data Sovereignty

Your data stays yours. That is not a policy position. It is the architecture.

Every organisation's data lives in its own Microsoft 365 tenancy, within Australian datacentres, under its own administrative control. Wirrah cannot reach it without explicit authorisation. When the contract ends, the infrastructure is decommissioned and no copy is retained by Wirrah. .

Location

Australian Microsoft datacentres only. Data sits within your tenancy, not Wirrah's and not GraceX's. Security infrastructure is provided and maintained by Microsoft under its enterprise compliance framework, including AES-256 encryption at rest and in transit.

Ownership

Unconditional. Your organisation owns all data generated through the platform, and may use it for any purpose, including as evidence in future funding applications, grant acquittals, or impact reporting. Consistent with Maiam nayri Wingara Indigenous Data Sovereignty principles (2018).

Access

Your administrator controls all access. Wirrah's advisory panel accesses only what is required for report preparation, via a provisioned guest account removed at contract end.

End of contract

Wirrah decommissions the infrastructure. No data is retained, archived, or held by Wirrah or GraceX after the contract ends.
